## 1. INTERNATIONAL / MULTILATERAL FUNDERS
*Available to eligible businesses in multiple countries*

### International Finance Corporation (IFC) — World Bank Group
- **What:** Equity, loans, trade finance for emerging market SMEs
- **Amount:** $1M+ (too large for most startups — better for scale-up)
- **Products:** Working capital, trade finance, supply chain finance
- **Website:** ifc.org/trade
- **Best for:** Established businesses scaling export operations

### International Trade Centre (ITC) — UNCTAD/WTO
- **What:** Technical assistance, market intelligence, capacity building
- **Amount:** Non-financial (training, market access, buyer connections)
- **Website:** intracen.org
- **Best for:** Market research, finding buyers, trade capacity building

### USAID / DFC (Development Finance Corporation)
- **What:** Loans, guarantees, equity for development impact businesses
- **Eligibility:** Developing country businesses or those creating impact there
- **Website:** dfc.gov
- **Best for:** Agribusiness, clean energy, women-owned enterprises

### African Development Bank (AfDB)
- **What:** Financing via local banks; SME programs via funds
- **Programs:** Affirmative Finance Action for Women in Africa (AFAWA), ENABLE Youth
- **Website:** afdb.org
- **Best for:** African businesses — food/agro, infrastructure, manufacturing

### Asian Development Bank (ADB)
- **What:** Trade Finance Program, SME financing via partner banks
- **Website:** adb.org/what-we-do/sectors/trade-supply-chain
- **Best for:** Asian businesses in emerging markets

### European Investment Bank (EIB) / European Investment Fund (EIF)
- **What:** Loans through local banks; guarantee programs for SMEs
- **Eligibility:** EU-based businesses or EU-linked development projects
- **Website:** eib.org | eif.org
- **Best for:** European SMEs, social enterprises

### Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF)
- **What:** $5,000 non-refundable seed capital + mentoring + training
- **Eligibility:** African entrepreneurs — any sector
- **Application:** Once per year (January)
- **Website:** tonyelumelufoundation.org
- **Best for:** Early-stage African entrepreneurs with no capital

### Mastercard Foundation
- **What:** Grants for youth employment, agriculture, financial inclusion
- **Eligibility:** African youth-focused businesses
- **Website:** mastercardfdn.org

### GIZ (German Development Cooperation)
- **What:** Technical assistance, grants for development-linked trade
- **Website:** giz.de
- **Best for:** Agribusiness, renewable energy, social enterprise

## 7. HOW TO CHOOSE THE RIGHT FUNDING TYPE

| Stage | Best Funding Type | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch, no revenue | Grant / TEF / angel | No repayment pressure during setup |
| First inventory purchase | Trade finance / working capital loan | Short-term, self-liquidating |
| Equipment purchase | Term loan / equipment finance | Repaid over useful life of asset |
| Export growth | Export credit / EXIM bank | Designed specifically for this |
| Scaling to new markets | Equity investment | Need capital + expertise + network |
| Managing buyer risk | Trade credit insurance | Protects revenue, not capital |
| Letter of Credit funding | LC-backed bank facility | Banks fund against confirmed LC |

**Matching funding to stage:**
- Seed / pre-launch: grants, TEF, personal savings, family
- Startup (Year 1): EXIM bank working capital, government SME loans, revolving credit
- Growth (Year 2–3): development bank term loans, equity investor, trade finance facility
- Scale (Year 3+): commercial bank, private equity, bond (if large enough)

## 8. FUNDING APPLICATION TIPS

### What Funders Always Want to See
1. **Business registration** — must be formally registered before applying
2. **Bank statements** — 6–12 months of business account history
3. **Business plan** — use DOC 2 (Business Strategy Brief)
4. **Financial projections** — use DOC 3 (Financial Model)
5. **Evidence of market demand** — LOIs, purchase orders, buyer contacts
6. **Management profile** — your experience, your team's credentials
7. **Collateral** — property, equipment, stock (for secured loans)
8. **Purpose of funds** — specific use, not vague ("for business")

### Common Reasons Applications Are Rejected
- No audited accounts or financial history (for loans > $50K)
- Projections not supported by evidence or assumptions
- No clear repayment plan (for loans)
- Missing documentation (incomplete applications always rejected)
- Business not registered or tax-non-compliant
- No skin in the game — funders want to see your own money in too
- Asking for too much relative to business size

### Sequencing Tip
Apply for grants first (no repayment), then concessionary loans (low rate),
then commercial loans (normal rate). Layer them — a grant + a government loan
+ a commercial facility is a common successful structure.

### The Best Single Action Before Applying
Get your business formally registered, open a business bank account, and
keep it active for 6+ months before applying for any significant loan.
Banks and funders use bank statement patterns as the primary risk signal.
