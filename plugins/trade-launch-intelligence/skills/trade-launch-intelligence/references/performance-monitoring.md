after launch. Use it every month to compare actual performance against
your DOC 3 projections, catch problems early, and make scaling decisions
with data — not instinct.

## 2. KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs) BY FUNCTION

### Financial KPIs

| KPI | How to Calculate | Healthy Range | Action if Outside Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gross Margin % | (Revenue - COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100 | Product-dependent (see DOC 3 targets) | Review pricing and COGS drivers |
| Net Margin % | Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 | > 10% (minimum) | Cut OpEx or raise prices |
| Cash Conversion Cycle | Days Inventory + Days Receivable - Days Payable | < 45 days | Collect faster, pay slower |
| Revenue vs. Plan % | Actual ÷ Plan × 100 | > 80% | Investigate shortfall |
| Monthly Burn Rate | Total cash out per month | Must be < monthly cash in | Immediate cost review |
| Runway | Cash in bank ÷ Monthly burn | > 3 months at all times | Emergency capital raise |

### Sales KPIs

| KPI | Healthy Range | Warning Level |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer conversion rate | > 20% of approached | < 10% |
| Repeat order rate | > 50% of buyers order again | < 30% |
| Average order size trend | Growing month on month | Declining |
| Buyer concentration | No buyer > 40% of revenue | Any buyer > 60% |
| Pipeline value | > 2× monthly revenue target | < 1× target |

### Supply Chain KPIs

| KPI | Healthy Range | Warning Level |
|---|---|---|
| QC pass rate (first time) | > 90% | < 80% |
| Supplier on-time delivery | > 85% | < 70% |
| Inventory days on hand | 30–45 days | < 14 days or > 60 days |
| Freight cost vs. budget | ± 10% | > 20% over budget |
| Destination rejection rate | < 1% | > 2% |

## 3. TRAFFIC LIGHT DECISION SYSTEM

Use this to convert monthly numbers into decisions quickly.

### 🟢 GREEN — On Track
Revenue ≥ 90% of plan AND gross margin ≥ plan AND cash positive.
**Action:** Continue. Look for acceleration opportunities.
Consider: increasing volume, entering a new market, hiring first staff.

### 🟡 AMBER — Watch Closely
Revenue 70–89% of plan OR margin 3–5 points below plan OR
cash balance declining but still positive.
**Action:** Investigate root cause. Do not change strategy yet.
Run a 30-day sprint to close the gap. Identify the one biggest lever.
Review: Is it a sales problem? A cost problem? A supply problem?

### 🔴 RED — Act Now
Revenue < 70% of plan OR negative net margin for 2+ consecutive months
OR cash runway < 6 weeks.
**Action:** Emergency review. See Pivot Decision Framework below.
Do not wait. Every week of inaction in a 🔴 situation costs more.

### ⚫ BLACK — Crisis
Cash runway < 3 weeks. Business cannot meet obligations.
**Action:** See Failure/Exit Protocol (separate file).
Stop taking new orders until financial position is stabilised.

## 4. QUARTERLY DEEP REVIEW

Run this every 3 months in addition to monthly reviews.
Allow 2–3 hours. Invite key team member(s) if applicable.

### Q1 — 3-Month Review Agenda
1. **Financial performance vs. Q1 budget** — full 3-month P&L review
2. **Market intelligence update** — has price or demand changed since DOC 1?
3. **Customer review** — which buyers are worth keeping? Who to drop?
4. **Supplier review** — which suppliers are performing? Any to replace?
5. **Competitive landscape update** — any new competitors? Any gaps to exploit?
6. **Certification status** — any renewals due in next 3 months?
7. **Strategy review** — is the original DOC 2 strategy still correct?
8. **Budget reforecast** — update Q2 and Q3 targets based on actuals

### Quarterly Strategic Questions
- Are we serving the right markets? Should we enter a new one?
- Is our pricing competitive? Too low? Too high?
- Is our product offering right? Should we add a processed variant?
- Is our team sufficient? Do we need to hire?
- Is our capital structure right? Do we need more finance?
- What is the single biggest thing holding us back from doubling?

## 5. SCALING DECISION FRAMEWORK

Use this when business is performing well (🟢) and you're considering growth.

### Scale Signal Checklist
Before committing to scaling, all of the following should be true:
- [ ] Revenue has been at or above plan for 3+ consecutive months
- [ ] Gross margin is stable or improving
- [ ] You have at least 2 reliable suppliers and 3 active buyers
- [ ] You have at least 3 months of cash runway AFTER scaling investment
- [ ] You have confirmed demand (LOIs / repeat orders) to justify the scale
- [ ] Your operational capacity (storage, QC, logistics) can handle more volume
- [ ] All certifications and regulatory requirements are fully in place

**If any item above is ❌ — do NOT scale yet. Fix the gap first.**

### Scaling Options (in order of risk)

| Option | Capital Required | Risk | When to Choose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Increase volume with existing buyers | Low (working capital) | Low | First choice always |
| Enter a new export market | Medium | Medium | After 6+ months stable |
| Add a processed/value-added variant | Medium-High | Medium | After supply chain mastered |
| Set up own processing facility | High | High | After 12+ months and $100K+ revenue |
| Franchise / partner model | Medium | Medium | After proven playbook exists |

## 6. PIVOT DECISION FRAMEWORK

Use this when business is consistently in 🔴 for 2+ months.

### Step 1 — Diagnose the Root Cause

Run through this checklist honestly:

**Is it a market problem?**
- Is the product price declining and squeezing margins?
- Has demand shifted or dried up?
- Are buyers choosing a competitor consistently?

**Is it a supply/operations problem?**
- Is sourcing cost too high or unreliable?
- Is product quality inconsistent?
- Are logistics costs eating margin?

**Is it a sales problem?**
- Are you reaching the right buyers?
- Is your outreach (DOC 10–13) working?
- Do buyers respond but not convert?

**Is it a financial problem?**
- Were projections too optimistic?
- Is cash tied up in inventory too long?
- Is payment collection too slow?

### Step 2 — Choose a Response

| Diagnosis | Response Options |
|---|---|
| Market problem | Switch product / add value-added variant / change target market |
| Supply problem | Change supplier / change sourcing region / renegotiate |
| Sales problem | Rework outreach (DOC 10–13) / change target buyer profile / trade fair |
| Financial problem | Cut costs / raise capital / renegotiate payment terms |
| Multiple problems | See Failure/Exit Protocol |

### Step 3 — Set a 60-Day Recovery Sprint

Define ONE specific, measurable target: "If we don't achieve [X] by [date],
we will [escalate / pivot / exit]." Commit to it. Review in 60 days.

## 7. ANNUAL REVIEW CHECKLIST

At the end of each trading year, complete the following:

**Financial**
- [ ] Full-year P&L vs. DOC 3 Year 1 / 2 / 3 projections
- [ ] Update financial model with actuals — reforecast next year
- [ ] File tax returns (get accountant if needed)
- [ ] Review insurance coverage for next year

**Regulatory**
- [ ] Renew all expiring certifications (product safety, standards, export agency — per your country)
- [ ] Check for any regulatory changes in source or export countries
- [ ] Update compliance checklist (DOC 4) with current requirements

**Market**
- [ ] Re-run Phase 1 market intelligence (prices, market size)
- [ ] Update competitive landscape (new entrants? exits?)
- [ ] Review target export markets — still the right ones?

**Strategy**
- [ ] Review DOC 2 strategy — still valid? Needs updating?
- [ ] Set Year [N+1] targets
- [ ] Update DOC 7 Executive Master Summary with year-end position
- [ ] Update DOC 9 One-Page Snapshot with latest numbers

**Relationships**
- [ ] Review supplier relationships — strengths, weaknesses, alternatives
- [ ] Review buyer relationships — who's growing? Who's declining?
- [ ] Identify 5 new buyers or markets to target in the new year

**Trigger the skill's Change Management system (Phase 11) for any
significant updates resulting from the annual review.**
