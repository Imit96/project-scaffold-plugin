**Nigeria Step-by-Step Export Process:**
1. Register at CAC + get TIN from FIRS + open domiciliary account
2. Register with NEPC (₦50K–₦150K | 2–4 weeks) — mandatory for export
3. Obtain product cert: NAFDAC (food/cosmetics ₦50K–₦500K | 3–6 months), SON (manufactured ₦30K–₦200K), NAQS phytosanitary (agro, per shipment)
4. Engage pre-shipment inspector: SGS / Bureau Veritas / Cotecna (~1% cargo value)
5. Submit export declaration via NCS NICIS II system; pay export levy (0.5–1% FOB)
6. Book freight via BOFFA-registered forwarder; main ports: Apapa, Tin Can, Onne, Calabar
7. Submit Form NXP to bank (forex repatriation declaration)
8. Collect: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, BL/AWB, Certificate of Origin, all product certs, Insurance Certificate
9. Repatriate forex within 90 days of shipment (CBN regulation)

**10 Common Nigeria Export Mistakes:**
1. Shipping without LC or advance — buyer default is common
2. Wrong HS code — causes customs delays and wrong tariff
3. Not getting NAFDAC before shipping food — leads to rejection
4. Not repatriating forex within 90 days — CBN penalty
5. Using unregistered freight forwarders — cargo theft risk
6. No marine insurance — one rejected shipment wipes profit
7. Not confirming buyer's import restrictions before signing contract
8. Undervaluing on invoice — illegal, causes NCS penalties
9. Skipping pre-shipment inspection — buyer can reject entire shipment
10. No written supply contract with local suppliers — price disputes

## 7. UNIVERSAL EXPORT/IMPORT PROCESS

Regardless of country, every cross-border trade involves these steps:

### Step 1: Business Registration
Register a legal entity in your country. Get a tax ID. Open a business bank account.

### Step 2: Product Compliance
Ensure the product meets both your country's export standards and the destination country's import standards.

### Step 3: Find Your Agency
Register with your country's export promotion agency. They provide market intelligence, trade fair access, and often financial support.

### Step 4: Documentation Assembly
Every shipment needs: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Certificate of Origin, Bill of Lading or Airway Bill, relevant product certificates, Insurance Certificate.

### Step 5: Customs Declaration
File an export/import declaration with your customs authority. Pay any applicable duties or levies.

### Step 6: Freight & Logistics
Book with a licensed freight forwarder. Choose sea, air, road, or rail depending on product, urgency, and cost.

### Step 7: Payment
Use: Letter of Credit (LC) for new buyers, TT with advance for medium-trust, Open Account for established buyers. Always use cargo insurance.

### Step 8: Repatriation (for exporters)
Bring export proceeds back into the home country within the regulatory deadline.

## 11. INTERNATIONAL CERTIFICATION QUICK REFERENCE

For exporters targeting premium markets, these certifications unlock higher prices and buyer access.
Always verify current costs with the certifying body before quoting to a user.

### EU Organic Certification (EU Regulation 2018/848)
- **What:** Certifies products are grown/processed without synthetic pesticides, GMOs, or prohibited inputs
- **Required for:** Labelling product as "organic" in EU market — unlocks 2–5× price premium
- **Process:**
  1. Choose an EU-accredited certifier (see list below)
  2. Apply and submit farm/facility documentation
  3. Farm inspection (on-site visit by certifier)
  4. Conversion period: 2 years for annual crops, 3 years for perennials (if previously using chemicals)
  5. Annual renewal inspection
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months for first certification (if farm is already chemical-free, conversion may be waived or shortened)
- **Cost range:**
  | Farm/Facility Size | Annual Cert Cost (approx.) |
  |---|---|
  | Small farmer / 1–5 ha | €200–€600/year |
  | Medium operation / 5–50 ha | €600–€1,500/year |
  | Processing facility | €500–€2,000/year |
  | Full supply chain cert | €1,500–€5,000/year |
- **Accredited certifiers (work globally):**
  - Control Union Certifications — controlunion.com
  - Ecocert — ecocert.com
  - Kiwa BCS Öko-Garantie — kiwa.com
  - CERES — ceres-cert.com
  - IMO (Institute for Marketecology) — imo.ch
  - Soil Association (UK organic) — soilassociation.org
- **Price uplift:** Standard moringa FOB $3.5–$6/kg → Organic FOB $8–$30/kg

### USDA Organic Certification (NOP — National Organic Program)
- **What:** US organic standard — required to label "organic" in the US market
- **Who issues:** USDA-accredited certifiers (same bodies above, plus CCOF, Oregon Tilth, OTCO)
- **Cost:** $400–$2,000/year (farm) | $600–$3,000/year (processor)
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months | 3-year transition period if previously used prohibited substances
- **Note:** EU organic and USDA organic are mutually recognised via equivalency arrangement — one certification often covers both markets

### Fairtrade Certification
- **What:** Certifies fair prices paid to farmers; community development premiums
- **Who issues:** Fairtrade International — fairtrade.net | Flocert — flocert.net
- **Best for:** Coffee, cocoa, tea, bananas, sugar, cotton — strong consumer recognition in Europe
- **Cost:** €1,500–€4,000/year (producer group) | €2,000–€6,000/year (trader)
- **Price premium:** Fairtrade minimum price + $0.20–$0.50/kg development premium
- **Timeline:** 4–6 months

### Rainforest Alliance Certification
- **What:** Sustainability certification covering environment, social, economic criteria
- **Who issues:** Rainforest Alliance — rainforest-alliance.org
- **Best for:** Coffee, cocoa, tea, bananas, palm oil
- **Cost:** Variable — based on farm size; typically $500–$3,000/year
- **Timeline:** 3–6 months

### GlobalGAP Certification
- **What:** Good Agricultural Practices — mandatory for fresh produce to major EU retailers
- **Who issues:** Accredited certifiers globally — globalgap.org
- **Best for:** Fresh fruit, vegetables, herbs — essential for supermarket supply chains
- **Cost:** €400–€1,500/year (farm)
- **Timeline:** 2–4 months

### Halal Certification
- **What:** Certifies compliance with Islamic dietary law — required for food/beverage in Muslim-majority markets
- **Who issues:** Country-specific bodies; JAKIM (Malaysia), MUI (Indonesia), ESMA (UAE), IFANCA, HFCE
- **Best for:** Food, beverage, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals targeting UAE, Saudi, Malaysia, Indonesia, North Africa
- **Cost:** $500–$2,500/year
- **Timeline:** 4–8 weeks for existing products

### ISO 22000 / HACCP (Food Safety Management)
- **What:** International food safety management system standard
- **Who issues:** Accredited certification bodies (SGS, Bureau Veritas, Intertek, TÜV)
- **Required by:** Major European and US food manufacturers when sourcing ingredients
- **Cost:** $1,000–$5,000/year (depends on facility size)
- **Timeline:** 6–12 months to implement and certify

### Certification Strategy by Target Market

| Target Market | Priority Certifications |
|---|---|
| EU (food/supplements) | EU Organic + Phytosanitary + Certificate of Origin |
| EU (premium/retail) | EU Organic + Fairtrade or Rainforest Alliance + GlobalGAP |
| USA (supplements) | USDA Organic + FDA facility registration + COA lab tests |
