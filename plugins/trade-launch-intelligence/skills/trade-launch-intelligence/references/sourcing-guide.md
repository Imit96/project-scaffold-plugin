## 3. Supplier Due Diligence Checklist

Before committing to any supplier, verify the following:

### Identity & Business Verification
- [ ] Visit supplier's physical location (never buy without visiting first)
- [ ] Verify CAC registration number at cac.gov.ng
- [ ] Confirm they have a functional storage facility
- [ ] Check references — speak to at least 2 previous buyers
- [ ] Confirm they have consistent supply volume (not seasonal speculators)

### Product Quality Verification
- [ ] Collect free samples and have them independently tested
- [ ] Check against SON or international grading standards for the product
- [ ] Verify moisture content, impurities, size grading as applicable
- [ ] Watch one loading process before committing large orders
- [ ] Ask for their quality rejection/return policy

### Financial Due Diligence
- [ ] Agree pricing formula (fixed price vs. index-linked)
- [ ] Confirm payment terms in writing
- [ ] Start with small order (1–5 tons) before scaling
- [ ] Never pay 100% upfront to unknown suppliers
- [ ] Get receipts/invoices for every transaction

### Supply Reliability Assessment
- [ ] Ask about their supply source (do they farm, aggregate, or broker?)
- [ ] Understand their peak vs. off-peak availability
- [ ] Confirm minimum and maximum supply capacity
- [ ] Ask how they handle supply shortfalls
- [ ] Check if they have other committed buyers (supply competition risk)

### Legal & Contractual
- [ ] Sign a Supply Agreement (even simple one-pager)
- [ ] Include: price, volume, quality spec, delivery point, payment terms
- [ ] Include force majeure clause
- [ ] Define penalty for supply failure
- [ ] Include dispute resolution mechanism

| TradeKey | Middle East, Pakistan, Bangladesh | Free | |

| PANJIVA | US import/export data | Paid | Find who's buying what globally |
| ImportGenius | Shipping records data | Paid | Identify real importers |
| Made-in-China.com | China alternatives to Alibaba | Free browse | |
| DHgate | Smaller orders from China | Free | |

### Tips for Using International Sourcing Platforms
1. Always request samples before bulk order
2. Use escrow/trade assurance payment where available
3. Verify factory/supplier via third-party inspection (SGS, Bureau Veritas)
4. Request ISO/quality certifications
5. Check supplier transaction history and buyer reviews
6. Negotiate on MOQ — almost always flexible
7. Understand lead times before committing to buyers

## 5. Price Discovery Methods

To know if you're getting a fair price at source:

### For Agro Commodities
- **AFEX Commodity Exchange** (afexnigeria.com) — Daily spot prices for Nigerian commodities
- **NCAM** (National Centre for Agricultural Mechanization) — Crop price bulletins
- **FAO GIEWS** (fao.org/giews) — Global commodity price data
- **ITC Trade Map** (trademap.org) — Export/import prices by country
- **Tridge.com** — Global agri-food price intelligence (partially free)
- **World Bank Commodity Markets** — Quarterly price outlooks

### For Manufactured Goods
- Compare minimum 3 supplier quotes
- Check landed cost calculators
- Factor in tariffs (customs.gov.ng duty calculator or WTO tariff database)

### For Minerals
- London Metal Exchange (LME) for metals
- USGS Mineral Resources for global pricing

## 6. Quality Grading Standards

### Cashew Nuts (Raw)
- Grade: KOR (Kernel Output Ratio) — higher is better
- Good grade: KOR 48–52+ lbs/80kg bag
- Moisture: Max 9%
- Foreign matter: Max 2%
- International standard: AFCC (African Cashew Alliance standards)

### Sesame Seeds
- Good grade: 99.95% purity
- Moisture: Max 6%
- Oil content: Min 50%
- Color: White (premium) or mixed
- Reference: USDA sesame standards

### Cocoa Beans
- Grade 1 (premium): Less than 5% defects
- Grade 2: 5–10% defects
- Moisture: Max 7.5%
- Fermentation: 75%+ fully fermented
- Reference: ICCO grading standards, Nigerian Cocoa Export Standard

### Shea Butter (Processed)
- Grade A (unrefined, crude): FFA < 5%, moisture < 0.2%
- Grade B (refined): FFA < 1%, white/ivory color
- Reference: AOCS standards for shea

### Palm Oil
- Special Quality: FFA < 1.5%, moisture < 0.1%
- Standard: FFA < 3.5%, moisture < 0.25%
- Reference: CODEX Alimentarius, MPOB standards

## 7. Supply Chain Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Supplier default | Medium | Multi-supplier strategy (min 3 suppliers), small advance |
| Price spike at source | High | Forward contracts, inventory buffer |
| Quality failure | Medium | Pre-purchase sample testing, on-site inspection |
| Transport delay | High | Build 2-week buffer in logistics planning |
| Theft in transit | Medium | Marine/cargo insurance, GPS tracking |
| Adulteration/fraud | Medium | Accredited lab testing on arrival |
| Seasonal supply gap | High | Buy and store during peak, use warehouse receipt finance |
| FX loss on payment | High | Collect in forex, convert at best rate, hedge if possible |

### Multi-Supplier Strategy
Never rely on one supplier. Maintain:
- Primary supplier: 60–70% of volume
- Secondary supplier: 20–30% of volume
- Emergency supplier: 10% backup (higher cost but available)

## 8. Sourcing Contracts & Agreements

### Key Clauses to Include in Supplier Agreement

**Pricing Clause:**
"The price per metric ton of [Product] shall be [₦X] fixed / or linked to [market index] ± [Y]% at time of order confirmation."

**Quality Clause:**
"All goods shall conform to [standard, e.g., AFCC Grade 1 / SON NIS XXX] and the buyer reserves the right to reject any consignment failing to meet agreed specifications, with full refund of purchase price."

**Delivery Clause:**
"Supplier shall deliver [X] metric tons to [location] within [Y] days of order placement, at supplier's cost."

**Force Majeure:**
"Neither party shall be liable for delay caused by natural disaster, government action, or other events beyond reasonable control, provided [7 days] written notice is given."

**Dispute Resolution:**
"Any dispute shall first be resolved through negotiation within [14 days]. Failing this, parties submit to arbitration under Nigerian Arbitration and Conciliation Act."
